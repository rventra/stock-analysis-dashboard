Meta Platforms finds itself at a crossroads — delivering exceptional fundamental results while facing significant technical and sentiment headwinds. The social media giant's stock has declined 12.75% over the past six months, raising questions about whether this represents a buying opportunity or the beginning of a longer correction.

The company continues to dominate the social media landscape with Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp, generating $200.97 billion in 2025 revenue — up 22.2% year-over-year. Profitability remains robust with EBITDA margins exceeding 52%. Yet despite these strong fundamentals, the stock trades at a reasonable forward P/E of just 18.4.

So what's holding the stock back? And more importantly — should you buy, hold, or sell? Let's dig into both sides of the argument.

Bull case:

Meta's fundamental story remains compelling. The company operates at the intersection of social media dominance and artificial intelligence leadership — two of the most powerful trends in technology. Here's why the bull case is worth considering:

  • Exceptional revenue growth: 2025 revenue reached $200.97 billion, representing 22.2% year-over-year growth. In the competitive technology landscape, few companies can match this scale combined with this growth rate.
  • Industry-leading profitability: EBITDA of $105.71 billion translates to a remarkable 52.6% margin. This cash generation capability provides significant flexibility for investment, dividends, and share repurchases.
  • Attractive valuation: Trading at a forward P/E of 18.4 and price-to-sales ratio of 8.3, Meta is cheaper than its historical averages despite maintaining dominant market positions. The multiple compression presents an opportunity for patient investors.
  • AI leadership position: Meta's investments in AI infrastructure, including its $14 billion+ partnerships with CoreWeave, position the company for long-term growth. The company's AI-powered advertising and content recommendation systems drive engagement and monetization.
  • Strong balance sheet: With a net cash position and low debt, Meta has the financial flexibility to weather economic headwinds and continue investing in growth opportunities.
  • Dominant ecosystem: Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Threads create an unmatched social media ecosystem with over 3 billion monthly active users across platforms.

Bear case:

Despite the strong fundamentals, several factors weigh on the stock. Technical analysis reveals significant weakness, and market dynamics have shifted against growth stocks. Here's why the bear case deserves attention:

  • Technical breakdown: Meta is trading below its 200-day moving average and has formed a death cross — a classic bearish technical signal where the 50-day SMA crosses below the 200-day SMA. This suggests further downside could be imminent.
  • MACD sell signal: The Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator shows -3.54, well below the signal line, indicating momentum has turned negative.
  • AI spending concerns: Meta's $14 billion+ AI infrastructure commitments, including deals with CoreWeave, have raised concerns about capital intensity and potential margin pressure in the near term.
  • Sector rotation: Growth stocks, particularly communication services names, have fallen out of favor as investors rotate toward value and defensive sectors amid economic uncertainty.
  • Multiple compression: Despite strong fundamentals, Meta's valuation has compressed as the market reprices growth stocks. This reflects broader concerns about the sustainability of tech spending.
  • Regulatory headwinds: Ongoing regulatory scrutiny of Big Tech companies creates uncertainty around future business practices and potential antitrust actions.
  • Modest dividend: With a yield of just 0.32% and an 8.9% payout ratio, income investors have limited reason to hold the stock for dividends alone.
